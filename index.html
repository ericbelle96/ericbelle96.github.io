Research

Job Market Paper

Dynamic Efficiency and Strategic Bidding in Common-Pool Resource Buybacks: Evidence from the Texas Inshore Shrimp Fishery

This paper studies how rival removal in common-pool resource buyback programs generates spillovers that alter remaining agents' valuations and reduce future program participation. Using data on 469 vessels in the Texas inshore shrimp fishery over 14 years, I estimate a structural spatial model of harvest effort allocation and map continuation values to bid functions, showing that removing rivals increases the continuation values of remaining fishers and decreases their willingness to exit through the buyback program.
